Wine Pairing Recommendations

1. Château d'Yquem, Sauternes, 2015

Château d'Yquem is a premier cru superieur (first great growth) wine from the Sauternes region in Bordeaux, France. The 2015 vintage is known for its rich sweetness, complex flavors of honey, apricot, and tropical fruits, and a long, lingering finish.

2. Dom Pérignon Vintage Champagne, 2008

Dom Pérignon Vintage Champagne from the renowned Champagne house Moët & Chandon. The 2008 vintage is celebrated for its exquisite balance, with notes of citrus, white flowers, and brioche. It has a lively acidity and a creamy texture.

3. Opus One, Napa Valley, 2016

Opus One is a Bordeaux-style blend from Napa Valley, California. The 2016 vintage is characterized by its opulent dark fruit flavors, velvety tannins, and hints of cedar and spice. It's a harmonious and well-structured red wine.

4. Barolo Riserva, Giacomo Conterno, 2013

Giacomo Conterno's Barolo Riserva is a classic Italian red wine from the Piedmont region. The 2013 vintage showcases Nebbiolo grape's elegance, with aromas of roses, tar, and red berries. It has a firm structure and a long, powerful finish.

5. Château d'Esclans, Garrus, Rosé, 2019

Château d'Esclans' Garrus is a premium rosé wine from Provence, France. The 2019 vintage offers a pale pink color, delicate floral aromas, and flavors of ripe strawberries and citrus. It's a sophisticated and refreshing rosé.

6. Krug Grande Cuvée, Champagne, NV

Krug Grande Cuvée is a non-vintage Champagne known for its exceptional quality. Crafted by the prestigious House of Krug, it features a blend of multiple vintages, offering a rich and complex profile with notes of toasted bread, nuts, and vibrant acidity.

Wine Recommendation

For the ultimate experience, we recommend the 2005 Château Margaux from Bordeaux, France. This exceptional red wine is a blend of Cabernet Sauvignon, Merlot, Cabernet Franc, and Petit Verdot. With its notes of black currant, violets, and cedar, it perfectly complements the rich flavors of the degustation menu.
